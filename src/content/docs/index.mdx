# Daisy

<div class="home-hero">
  <span class="home-kicker">One Ecosystem, Two Products</span>

  <p>DaisyCore and DaisySeries share one brand surface, one docs site, and one design system, while staying separate products with clean dependency choices.</p>

<div class="value-strip">

  <div class="value-pill"><strong>DaisySeries</strong><span>The lightweight parser toolkit for materials, sounds, item flags, canonical keys, and friendly display names.</span></div>

## Choose your product

<div class="home-grid">
  <div class="home-card">
    <h3>DaisyCore</h3>
    <p>Choose DaisyCore when your plugin needs a coherent Paper platform with bootstrap ownership, commands, menus, sidebars, tablists, shared text, placeholders, and items.</p>
    <p><strong>Best for:</strong> full plugin systems, UI flows, runtime ownership, and one-dependency platform adoption.</p>
    <p><a href="/daisycore/">Open DaisyCore docs</a></p>
  </div>
  <div class="home-card">
    <h3>DaisySeries</h3>
    <p>Choose DaisySeries when you want modern enum parsing helpers without adopting a full framework. It focuses on config-safe parsing, friendly names, and canonical serialization keys.</p>
    <p><strong>Best for:</strong> lightweight plugins, config-heavy parsing, and enum utility layers you can use anywhere.</p>
    <p><a href="/daisyseries/">Open DaisySeries docs</a></p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="api-note">
  <p><strong>DaisyCore</strong> can own your platform lifecycle and UI systems.</p>
